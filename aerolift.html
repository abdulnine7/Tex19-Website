AEROLIFT (RC Plane Competition)

Details

Max. No. Of Participants: 5 per team

Registration Fees: Rs 500/-

No. of Rounds: 2

ABOUT

  • Participants must design and build their own aircraft. Use of ready to fly (RTF) kit and almost ready to fly kits are strictly not allowed. Multirotors not allowed.
  • Only electric powered RC planes are allowed. Use of IC engine is prohibited.
  • Competing designs are limited to fixed wing aircrafts only. No lighter-than-air or rotary wing aircraft such as helicopter or autogyros will be allowed to compete.
  • Participants are free to use any material of their choice. Participants are advised to take special safety precaution of their aircraft. Organizers will not be responsible for any damage caused to the model or its electrical component during the event.
  • Participants must be present at the event site before or at the reporting time. Teams arriving late will be disqualified.
  • Maximum five participants are allowed in each team. Team members can be from same or different institutes.
  • However, one participant may not be a part of multiple teams for the same event.
  • Judges and organizers decision on any matter will be final and binding to all.
  • Propeller size should not exceed 14 inches.
  • College ID is compulsory.
  • Use of gyroscope or any autopilot system is strictly prohibited, if found the team will be disqualified.
     

Design Objective

  • To design an aircraft that can lift as much payload as possible while observing available power and aircraft’s wingspan requirements.
  • The competition will be divided into 2 phases (PHASE-1 & PHASE-2)
    • Phase 1: Design Synopsis
      • Teams will electronically submit basic dimensions of their aircraft. If the aircraft manufacturing is done before submitting the synopsis, pictures of manufacturing should also be provided.
    • Phase 2: Flight Round
      • Flight Round will consist of two rounds Round 1 and Round 2.
  • Teams which qualify 1st will take part in the 2nd round.

Aircraft Dimension Requirement:

  • The Aircraft shall have a maximum wingspan of 1.2 meters.
  • The Aircraft should not weigh more than 1.5 kg excluding payload.
     

Mission Requirements

Round Attempt

Teams are allowed two (2) flight attempt per round.

  • As soon as team enters the arena the Aircraft should be airborne within 5 minutes. Teams which fail to airborne their aircraft within 5 minutes will be disqualified.
  • Timer will start from the moment the team enters the arena.
  • The Aircraft must be hand launched.
  • Best of two flight attempts will be considered as the respective score of the round.

Payload Requirements:

  • The payload will not be provided by the organizers and the participating team must bring their own payload.
  • The payload should not contribute to aircrafts structural strength or stability.

Aircraft Ballast:

  • Aircraft ballast is allowed to be used as team’s desire with the following exceptions:
  • Ballast will never be counted as payload.

Aircraft Takeoff and Circuit:

  • Takeoff is defined as the point at which the aircraft departs the hand of the person throwing the aircraft. Once takeoff occurs, aircraft is required to;
    • For Round 1: Remain airborne and form a circle between given two turn points (pole).
    • For Round 2: Remain airborne and form a figure of ‘8’ between given two turn points (pole).
    • The aircraft must take off and land intact to receive points for the flight. All parts must remain attached to the aircraft during flight and during the landing maneuver. Broke propellers are allowed, and will not invalidate a flight attempt.

Competition Scoring:

  • For Round 1 and Round 2:
  • Score = Payload Fraction * 1000
  • (Payload fraction= Total weight of Aircraft ÷ Empty weight of the aircraft)
  • (Total weight = Aircrafts Empty weight + Payload)
  • Total score = Score of round 1 + Score of round 2

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:

Any student with valid ID card of their educational institute can participate

** Prize money may be subjected to change. Organisers reserve the right to change any rules.
