HYDROLOGICS

Details

Max. No. Of Participants: 3 per team

Registration Fees: Rs 500/- per team

No. of Rounds: 2

EVENT DETAILS:

  • Build a Hydraulic arm using syringes, pipes and water as fluid that can lift certain objects and transfer them to desired positions.
  • Maximum Degree of freedom can be 5.
  • The hydraulic arm should be able to transfer solid object(s) from source to destination.

    • EVENT FORMAT:

    • ROUND I (Building the arm) :
      • 90 minutes will be provided to build the hydraulic arm from scratch. Materials (specifically syringes and tubes) will be provided by the organisers.
      • The body of the arm must be prepared from cardboard only.
      • Cardboard and other required elements should be brought by the participants.
      • Teams unable to complete the arm in specified time will be eliminated.
      • Completed arms will be tested wherein they’ll have to lift a block of certain weight through a certain distance.
      • Teams completing the test run are eligible for Round II.
    • ROUND II (Knockout) :
      • This is a one on one tournament. Teams will be paired up through random draws.
      • The team with more number of points in a stipulated amount of time of 20 minutes will advance.
    • BATTLE ARENA:
      • The battle arena will be a 120cm x 60cm rectangular area that will be divided into two.
      • Each team will have 60cm x 60cm area and the adjoining area will be of other team.
      • Each team will have 8 green blocks and 8 yellow boxes in their own arena.
      • Green boxes are sized at 6x6x6 sq. cm. and weigh around 100 g, whereas yellow ones are sized at 4x4x4 sq. cm. and weigh around 60 g.
      • These boxes are to be put in vertical containers placed at different distance and heights.
      • Green containers will be placed at a distance of 20 cm from the arena and will tower at a height of 30 cm.
      • Yellow containers will be placed at a distance of 15 cm from the arena at a height of 15 cm.
      • Each correct placing of a green block will give the participants 30 points and a yellow block will give 20 points.
      • In case of a tie, team with more number of green blocks wins.
      • A red coloured sphere will also be placed in the centre of the arena that will be common to both the teams. It has a radius of 4cm and weighs 120g.
      • This sphere is to be put in a red container placed 25 cm from the arena towering at 25 cm.
      • Pocketing the red ball gives 50 points.
      • In case of a tie where the number of green blocks is also same, the team which pockets the red sphere wins.
      • A team can’t pocket the red ball until it has at least 4 boxes pocketed.
    • OTHER RULES :
      • Any team seen misusing or wasting the given material shall be disqualified.
      • Only failures during the competition will be entertained, in case of a failure during making of the arm, no extra material will be supplied.
      • If there is a failure during the knockout round, the team will be given a time of 15 minutes to rectify the problem. If they are unable to resolve the error, it is up to them to either continue with the problem or give up the match.
      • In case of a second error, the match will continue without a halt.
      • Teams will be given sufficient time before the match to check on the condition of their hydraulic arms.
    • ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:
      • Any student with valid ID card of their educational institute can participate.
      • Each participant can be a part of one team only. If same participant is found as a member of more than one team, will lead to disqualification of both teams.

    Any changes and layout of the arena will be displayed beforehand on the Texephyr website (www.texephyr.in).
